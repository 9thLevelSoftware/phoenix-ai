# Safety Boundaries & Digital Spotting

Because the Vitruvian Trainer generates highly responsive electromagnetic force capable of delivering intense loads instantly, physical and digital safety guardrails must be rigorously enforced.

## 1. Digital Spotting (Active Safety)
The Vitruvian Trainer contains a real-time computerized spotter that acts immediately to protect the user:
- **Velocity-Triggered Spotter**: If the concentric velocity of a repetition drops below **0.15 m/s** (stalling) or if a sudden downward velocity is detected during a concentric phase, the system instantly decreases the load to the minimum **8 lbs (3.6 kg)** tracking tension.
- **Range of Motion (ROM) Spotter**: If the user drops the handle/bar below their starting calibrated range of motion, the motor immediately unloads.
- **Engagement Trigger**: Weight only loads after the user performs a full, stable, unloaded range-of-motion calibration (usually 3 unloaded calibration reps).

## 2. Equipment Setup & Cable Safety
- **Cable Inspection**: Users should inspect cables before every session. Any signs of fraying, sheath cracking, or uneven cable spooling require immediate machine shutdown and cable replacement.
- **Safe Starting Weights**: For any new exercise, start at the minimum **8 lbs tension per cable** or a light weight to calibrate range of motion before ramping up to heavy loads.
- **Base Attachment Stability**: Ensure all attachment clips (handles, bar, ankle straps) are fully locked into the cable connector hooks before starting. Never release a loaded cable from your hands; always guide it smoothly back to the machine base.

## 3. Physical Environmental Safety
- **Clear Workspace**: Ensure a 1.5-meter radius around the Vitruvian platform is clear of any objects, walls, or furniture to avoid accidental impact.
- **Foot Placement**: Always stand centered on the platform. The platform uses your body weight to anchor itself against the heavy upward forces generated by the motors.
