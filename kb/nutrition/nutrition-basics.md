# Nutrition Basics for Strength & Hypertrophy

Optimal body composition and muscle hypertrophy require an aligned nutritional strategy to fuel performance and support muscle protein synthesis.

## 1. Energy Balance (Calorie Targets)
- **Muscle Gain (Hypertrophy)**: A mild caloric surplus is highly recommended (**+250 to +500 kcal/day** above maintenance). This provides the energy required to synthesize new muscle tissue while minimizing excess fat accumulation.
- **Fat Loss (Cutting)**: A moderate caloric deficit is required (**-300 to -500 kcal/day** below maintenance) to mobilize adipose tissue while maintaining muscle mass through high protein intake and resistance training.

## 2. Macronutrient Guidelines

### Protein (The Structural Block)
- **Target**: **1.6 to 2.2 g per kilogram of bodyweight** (approx. **0.8 to 1.0 g per pound**) per day.
- **Timing**: Distribute protein intake relatively evenly across 3 to 5 meals per day (approx. 0.4-0.5g/kg per meal) to maximize muscle protein synthesis triggers.

### Carbohydrates (The Fuel)
- **Target**: **3 to 7 g per kilogram of bodyweight** per day, depending on training volume and intensity.
- **Role**: Primary fuel source for high-intensity resistance training. Restores muscle glycogen levels, preventing performance declines in subsequent training sessions.

### Fats (The Regulator)
- **Target**: **0.5 to 1.5 g per kilogram of bodyweight** per day (approx. 20-35% of total caloric intake).
- **Role**: Essential for hormone regulation (including testosterone production), cell membrane integrity, and absorption of fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K).
